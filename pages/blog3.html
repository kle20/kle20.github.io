The Trunk Test

#Tutorial

Steve Krug sure knows how to make a book digestable. And after reading his book Don't Make Me Think, I wanted to save a section I'd love to refer back to whenever I design a website. It's called the Trunk Test, a set of questions any end-user should be able to answer:

  1. What site is this?
  2. What page am I on?
  3. What are the major sections of this site?
  4. What are my options at this level?
  5. Where am I in the scheme of things?
  6. How can I search?

It's a quick way to make sure you aren't forgetting the essentials for a website. And guarantees that fundamental page elements are present in your design: the site ID, page name, page sections, local navigation, location indicators, and a search bar.

And while he likened it to being kidnapped, I compared it to something more relatable. When I was a kid, I'd like to wander around -- oftentimes away from sight of my parents. I'd find myself slightly lost and would immediately follow a set of questions to reorientate myself to find my parents. Following links and getting dropped into the middle of a website is much like that -- and less traumatizing compared to finding yourself tied in the back of a car.
