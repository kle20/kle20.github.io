Making the Portfolio

#Portfolio

I think if you want to see evidence of evolution, you should check the portfolio entry for "A Fueler's Map". It's an entry that wasn't prompted by the UX/UI class, but rather for Design Studio. I used a lot of tips and tricks I learned throughout this semester in that DS project -- particularly about stories and user feedback. Anyway, my portfolio isn't very relevant to my likely future career, so I chose some values that reflected that. I decided that my two values for my portfolio would be approachable and respect.

  • For being approachable, I decided to relax any serious, professional undertones. My writing is fairly casual and more of a narrative of my thoughts on the matter. I've also paid particular attention to white space within text, trying to avoid intimidating large blocks of text. And the longer posts -- portfolio entries and a study or two -- are also segmented by headers to make them more digestable. Additionally, I don't follow a particular color scheme besides purple for navigational items. That colorfulness for all my images helps the friendly, approachable value I want my portfolio to have.
  • For respect, I wanted to make sure that anyone who wanted to visit my portfolio was "treated well" so to say. (So I'm like being a good host!) This plays into having a quality interface; so anything clickable has some sort of signifier and all images have alt text. I'd say the organization of my portfolio shouldn't cause any frustrations either. Things are either divided into a portfolio entry or a blog post -- which I think is easy to understand and navigate. From a different end of respect, any material I've referenced is explicitly cited within my writing -- making sure they're credited properly (even if it's in a more casual way).
